GRADING   
In this course, students will be graded according to a 4-point, proficiency-based scale: 

10:    Thorough Understanding (purple)  
8.5:   Advancing Understanding (blue)  
7.5:   Emerging Understanding (yellow)  
5:      Not Enough Understanding (orange)

The goal for all students is to achieve understanding in all learning targets before moving on to the next assessment. Therefore, students are allowed to reassess **one time** on all learning targets within two weeks of receiving their grade. In order to retake, students must be up to date on their homework and submit corrections of their original assessment. Retakes are generally offered during flex periods. If students cannot make a scheduled retake, it is their responsibility to schedule an alternate time with the instructor at least 36 hours in advance. Retakes will be graded according to a **decaying average**: the original assessment will count for 30% of the final grade and the retake will count for 70%.

CLASSROOM EXPECTATIONS   
As your teacher...

* I organize and provide all materials and assignments using My Marlborough.  
* I clearly describe the learning goals for our units and lessons.  
* I give feedback \- whether it’s a grade or something else \- in a timely fashion and keep my gradebook up-to-date.  
* I give you a rubric for all of your graded tasks.  
* I will write at least three Official Notes home per year.

Student basics...

* I am on time to class with my materials.   
* I know how to check My Marlborough for assignments and class work.  
* I e-mail or talk to the teacher whenever I have a question or problem; I don’t wait until the night before an assignment or test.  
* I communicate absences with my teacher and I make a plan for the work that I missed within one rotation of my return. I understand that after that point, the late work policy applies. I check MyMarlborough for the work that I need to complete. I do not ask “What did I miss?”

As a  successful language learner, I aim high and act with integrity...

* I participate in the target language and avoid English.  
* I am kind and inclusive when my classmates and I work together.  
* I complete work with care (not sloppy, ripped, incomplete).   
* I identify and address areas for improvement (“I think I need more help with…”).  
* I trust myself to use new material actively, even if I make mistakes.  
* I help my classmates when I can.  
* I use material from previously learned topics and everyday expressions.  
* I recognize and respond to common words and expressions ("ça va?" "nĭ hăo?" , etc.).          
* I can figure out the main idea when reading and find examples (use context rather than guessing).  
* I correctly spell and use familiar words and expressions in my writing.  
* I can accurately use an online dictionary, such as Wordreference.  
* I understand the pitfalls of Google Translate and generative AI  and why I cannot use them.  
* I practice writing online or using other electronic devices (typing accents).  
* I know how to use and incorporate Comments/Suggestions on Google Docs and Slides.

WORK LOAD  
Students should expect to spend 20-30 minutes per rotation on homework for this class. If you find yourself consistently spending more than 45 minutes to an hour on homework, schedule a meeting with your teacher immediately for additional support. 

DEPARTMENT SPECIFIC POLICIES   
Students are expected to complete all graded and ungraded assignments independently. Tutors, friends, family, and adults that speak the language should not contribute to student work. Any questions about assignments should be directed to the teacher.
